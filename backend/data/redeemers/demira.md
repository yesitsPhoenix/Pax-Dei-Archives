The Third Redeemer, Demira, rose during the Age of the Ancients, between 876-1050. She brought agriculture, care, and craft.

> Demira gave strength to the body and gentleness to the hand.
>
> She taught us to till the earth, to plant and reap, to tend to beasts not for domination but companionship.
>
> Yet her greatest gift was care itself, a virtue that flowed into every act of tending, healing, and nurturing, be it child or soil, lamb or elder.
>
> Under her guidance we learned to build homes, raise herds, and create the first true communities.

Demira's breath brought about the season of Spring, and it was said that her tears produced the first Autumn rain, for before her, there was only Summer and Winter.She brought about seasons, giving the world birth and life in Spring and harvest and reflesion in Autumn.
